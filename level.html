Level

Principles & Devices

Level indicates the amount of material in a vessel. Methods include hydrostatic (pressure at depth), radar (FMCW/pulse), ultrasonic (time-of-flight), capacitive (dielectric changes), nuclear (gamma attenuation), and mechanical floats. Choose based on media, vapor, foam, and geometry.

MethodPrincipleBest ForNotes
Hydrostatic/DPρ·g·h pressureLiquidsNeed density compensation
Radar (FMCW)Microwave reflectionLiquids/solidsInsensitive to vapor/temp
UltrasonicSound time-of-flightLiquidsAffected by foam/vapor
CapacitanceDielectric changeLiquids/solidsProbe contact; coating effects
Point Level (Switches)Float/vibrating forkHigh/low alarmsSimple, robust
Mounting: Consider nozzle length, dead zones, and obstructions. Use stilling wells for turbulent tanks. For solids, pay attention to angle of repose and dust. Verify radar antenna selection and dielectric constants.
