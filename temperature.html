Temperature

Principles & Devices

Temperature sensors convert thermal energy to electrical signals. Common types: thermocouples (Seebeck voltage between dissimilar metals), RTDs (resistance changes in platinum), and thermistors (semiconductor resistance). Infrared pyrometers measure radiated energy for non-contact applications.

SensorPrincipleAccuracyRange
RTD (Pt100)Resistive (α≈0.00385 Ω/Ω/°C)±0.1–0.3 °C-200 to 600 °C
Thermocouple (Type K)Seebeck voltage±1–2 °C-200 to 1260 °C
Thermistor (NTC)SemiconductorHigh (narrow range)-50 to 150 °C
IR PyrometerThermal radiation±1–2 % of reading0 to 3000 °C (model dependent)
Installation: Use thermowells for process protection; consider response time, immersion length, and heat conduction. Match sensor type to transmitter input and include cold junction compensation for thermocouples.
