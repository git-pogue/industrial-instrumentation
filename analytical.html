Analytical

Principles & Devices

Analytical instruments quantify chemical properties: pH/ORP (electrochemical cells), conductivity (ionic mobility), dissolved oxygen (optical or Clark cell), turbidity (light scattering), and gas detection (electrochemical, catalytic bead, NDIR). Proper sample conditioning is critical.

ParameterPrincipleUse CasesNotes

ConductivityAC cell constant (κ)Purity monitoringChoose contacting/inductive type
Dissolved O₂Optical luminescenceAeration controlLow maintenance vs polarographic
TurbidityNephelometric scatteringFiltration performanceUnits NTU
Gas (NDIR)Infrared absorption bandsCO₂, hydrocarbonsRequires sampling system
Sample Systems: Include filtration, temperature/pressure control, flow, and calibration provisions to ensure representative measurements. Account for lag time and maintenance access.
