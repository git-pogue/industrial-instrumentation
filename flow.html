Flow

Principles & Devices

Flow instruments quantify volume or mass rate. Techniques include differential pressure (orifice, Venturi), electromagnetic (Faraday's law), Coriolis (mass flow via vibrating tube phase shift), vortex shedding, ultrasonic (transit-time or Doppler), and turbine.

MeterPrincipleBest ForNotes
Orifice Plate (DP)Bernoulli & ΔPLiquids/gases, high tempRequires compensation & straight-run
Mag MeterFaraday inductionConductive liquidsNo moving parts
CoriolisTube vibration phaseMass flow & densityHigh accuracy, higher cost
VortexVon Kármán vorticesSteam & gasesWide turndown
UltrasonicTransit-time/DopplerLarge pipes, clamp-onNon-intrusive options
Compensation: For DP flow, temperature & pressure compensation improves accuracy. Ensure upstream/downstream straight runs per standards; consider Reynolds number and fluid properties.
